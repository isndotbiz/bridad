## LEGAL CLOCK — DO NOT MISS

| Deadline | Trigger | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **HIPAA records: 30 days from request** | Federal | Document #3 must be sent within 7 days; follow up day 31 |
| **Oregon med-mal discovery rule: 2 years** | Date of injury / death | Attorney engaged within 60–90 days, suit filed within 18–24 months |
| **Oregon wrongful death: 3 years from date of death** | ORS 30.020 | Hard outer limit for the wrongful-death claim |
| **Oregon med-mal repose: 5 years from treatment** | ORS 12.110(4) | Beyond this, no claim regardless of discovery |
| **Oregon negligence repose: 10 years** | ORS 12.115 | Absolute outer limit |
| **WA med-mal: 3 years OR 1 year from discovery (longer)** | RCW 4.16.350 | Less likely to apply (treatment was in OR) but document it |
| **WA med-mal repose: 8 years** | RCW 4.16.350 | Outer limit if WA forum |

## What the family should NOT do

- **Do not post about it on social media** in detail. Anything posted becomes discoverable.
- **Do not sign anything** from Legacy or its insurers without the attorney reviewing.
- **Do not accept any "settlement" before records and autopsy are reviewed.** A pre-records settlement offer is almost always undervalued.
- **Do not throw away medications, prescription bottles, paperwork, or any of his belongings** that might be evidence — even if grief makes you want to clear it all out.
- **Do not let any family member talk to Legacy's risk management without the attorney present** once the litigation hold is in place. Polite cooperation pre-hold is fine; post-hold, route everything through counsel.

---

## What this is and what it isn't

This is **information gathering and evidence preservation**. It is what every family does after any sudden post-surgical death. It is not an accusation of negligence and it does not commit the family to suing.

The autopsy may show pulmonary embolism — a known and sometimes unavoidable complication, even with appropriate prophylaxis. It may show endocarditis with septic embolism — a missed diagnosis that an attorney will pursue hard. It may show an arrhythmia from a drug interaction — same. Or it may show something nobody expected.

**You don't get to make those choices in the dark.** This packet is what gets you the light to make them.

## When you're ready, but not before

- Probate / estate administration
- Death-benefit claims (life insurance, pension, Social Security survivor benefits, VA if applicable)
- Final tax filings
- Memorialization

These can wait. The autopsy decision and the records request cannot.
