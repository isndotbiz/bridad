# Medical Examiner Request — Demand Autopsy Investigation

**Send within 24 hours. Phone first. Document #1 (no-embalming) must already be in place.**

---

## Step 1 — Determine which county / state has jurisdiction

The Medical Examiner with jurisdiction is the one **where the death occurred**, NOT where treatment occurred.

- **If he died in Oregon (Multnomah County — Portland area):** Multnomah County Medical Examiner's Office — **(503) 988-0055** | medical.examiner@multco.us | 619 NW 6th Ave, 4th Floor, Portland, OR 97209.
- **If he died in Oregon (other county):** see [Oregon ME Jurisdiction Contacts directory](https://www.oregon.gov/oha/PH/BIRTHDEATHCERTIFICATES/Document%20Forms/MEJurisdictionContacts.pdf) — find the county.
- **If he died in Washington:** call the county coroner's or medical examiner's office where he was found. (King County: 206-731-3232; Pierce County: 253-798-6494; Snohomish: 425-438-6200; other counties: search "[County name] WA medical examiner".)

## Step 2 — Phone call first

Call the appropriate ME office. **Read this script:**

> "I'm calling about the death of [PATIENT FULL NAME], date of death [PATIENT DOD], at [LOCATION]. He was a 60-year-old man in good health overall, but had been undergoing 18 surgeries at Legacy Emanuel Medical Center in Portland for an external-fixator limb-reconstruction procedure with chronic infection complications. He was one week from final pin removal when he collapsed and died with no warning, no chest pain, no fever, nothing. **Under ORS chapter 146, this case appears to qualify for medical-examiner investigation as an unexpected death in apparent good health. I am formally requesting that your office perform an autopsy.** I am the [relationship], and I am also putting this request in writing. What is your case number, and what is the next step?"

Get: case number, name of investigator/deputy ME assigned, whether they will autopsy, expected timeline.

If they decline to autopsy, ask in writing for **the basis of the declination** — that document is useful later.

I am formally requesting that your office investigate this death and perform a complete autopsy, including external examination, internal examination, and **comprehensive toxicology**.

The death satisfies statutory criteria for medical-examiner investigation:

1. **Unexpected death in apparent good health.** [PATIENT FULL NAME] was 60 years old, generally healthy, and exhibited no symptoms — no fever, chest pain, shortness of breath, or distress — preceding his sudden collapse on [DATE].
2. **Death not under the immediate care of a physician at the time of death.** He was at home.
3. **Recent extensive surgical history with active infection.** He had completed 18 surgeries at Legacy Emanuel Medical Center for an external-fixator limb-reconstruction (Taylor Spatial Frame / Ilizarov technique) with chronic infection management. He was scheduled for final pin removal one week from the date of death.

The case warrants particular attention to:

- **Pulmonary embolism** — every classic risk factor was present (chronic immobility, recent surgery, infection-driven hypercoagulability, age 60).
- **Infective endocarditis with septic embolic events** — chronic indwelling-hardware bacteremia is a high-risk substrate; vegetation rupture and septic coronary embolism cause sudden death without prodrome.
- **Drug-induced arrhythmia (QT prolongation / torsades de pointes)** — should be evaluated with toxicology if antibiotics including azithromycin, fluoroquinolones, or co-administered ondansetron were on board, particularly with low potassium or magnesium. **Embalming will permanently destroy this evidence; toxicology must be drawn before any embalming.**
- **Acute coronary syndrome from chronic-inflammation-driven plaque destabilization** — coronary artery dissection at autopsy is the discriminator.

The remains are currently **refrigerated only, NOT embalmed**, at [FUNERAL HOME NAME], [PHONE]. The family will hold embalming for at least seven (7) days to allow autopsy.

If your office declines to perform the autopsy, please provide the basis for that decision in writing, so the family may proceed with a private autopsy.

## If the ME declines

The ME may classify the death as "natural" given his orthopedic history and decline to autopsy. The family does **not** have to accept that.

**Options for private autopsy:**

- **OHSU Department of Pathology** — Surgical Pathology line: **(503) 494-6776** (Mon–Fri 8 am–5 pm). After-hours / holidays: OHSU Paging Operator (503) 494-8211, ask for Pathology Resident on call. OHSU primarily autopsies its own admitted patients; ask whether they accept family-requested autopsies and what their fee is.
- **Private Autopsy, Inc.** — nationwide forensic-pathologist network — [privateautopsy.com](https://www.privateautopsy.com/). Cost typically $2,000–$5,000+ depending on toxicology, histology, neuropath.
- **Hsu Enterprise / privateautopsy.net** — alternate independent service.

**Key questions to ask any private pathologist:**

1. What is the all-in cost? Does it include toxicology, histology, microbiology, and a written report?
2. How quickly can they perform the autopsy? (Goal: within 72 hours of death.)
3. Will they testify if the case becomes a legal matter? (Some won't.)
4. How long for the preliminary report (typical: 24–48 hours after exam)? How long for the final (typical: 60–90 days)?
5. Will toxicology specifically include the antibiotic class on board (verify they have the assays for fluoroquinolones, macrolides, and ondansetron levels)?
