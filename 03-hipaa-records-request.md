# HIPAA Right-of-Access Records Request + Litigation Hold — Legacy Emanuel

**Send certified mail, return receipt requested, this week.** Filed by the personal representative.

Background: under HIPAA, a personal representative of a deceased patient has the same right of access as the patient. PR right-of-access requests are subject to the federal "reasonable cost-based fee" cap (typically ~$6.50 flat fee per request) and a 30-day federal turnaround — patient-friendlier than third-party authorizations.

This litigation hold supersedes any document-retention or auto-deletion policy. Failure to preserve may result in spoliation sanctions.

## Notes for the family (do not include)

- **You can send this without an attorney.** It is not a lawsuit. It is a federally-protected information request plus a routine litigation-preservation notice.
- **The litigation-hold language is the load-bearing part.** It legally prevents Legacy from auto-deleting EHR audit logs (which routinely roll off after a fixed retention period). Without this notice, evidence may evaporate.
- **If Legacy delays past 30 days**, file a HIPAA right-of-access complaint with the **HHS Office for Civil Rights** at hhs.gov/hipaa/filing-a-complaint — it is free and effective.
- **If they charge you more than ~$10**, push back citing 45 C.F.R. § 164.524(c)(4) and HHS 2016 guidance.
- **Send this same litigation-hold notice to any other facility where he was treated** — the surgeon's clinic, any imaging center, any rehab facility, any home-health agency.
